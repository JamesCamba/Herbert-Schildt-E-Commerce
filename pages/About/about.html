Biography & Background

Herbert Schildt is a renowned American author, programmer, and musician, best known for his books on programming languages. His works have sold millions of copies worldwide, making him one of the most widely read technical authors in computer programming.

  • Education: He holds both undergraduate and graduate degrees from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC).
  • Programming Contributions: Schildt claims to have participated in the original ANSI committee that standardized the C language in 1989, as well as the ANSI/ISO committees for C++ and C in the 1990s. His involvement in these committees has been noted, though some peers have debated the extent and accuracy of his contributions.
  • Career: Schildt is primarily recognized for making programming accessible through his clear and structured writing. He focuses on languages like C, C++, Java, and C#, producing comprehensive reference books and beginner guides.
